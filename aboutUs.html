The Namwera Project - Community Service with a Global Reach. How a Jamaican- Canadian woman from Toronto established a school in a rural village in Malawi, Africa. The value of volunteering, the broadening of life's experiences and the power of giving back.
Kamala Jean Gopie is a retired elementary school teacher who taught in East York and North York. She was also an Educator Officer at the Ontario Ministry of Education. After retirement from education, she was appointed as a member of the Immigration and Refugee Board where for 8 years she made refugee determinations. She was subsequently appointed a part-time Ontario Human Rights Commissioner for two years. For over forty years she has been a volunteer working with community, arts, education, social service, health and funding organizations such as the Jamaican Canadian Association, Black Business and Professional Association, Roy Thomson Hall, Obsidian Theatre, Governing Council of the University of Toronto, and the United Way of Greater Toronto. Based on her community service she was awarded the Order of Ontario.

Her current focus is a project she started in Malawi. A school has built in a village. Pre- school children, women and young men now have the opportunity to get an education. The women are learning to read and write English as well learn to sew and the young men who dropped out of school, are upgrading their skills and operate a maize mill as a way to earn an income. With recent flooding in Malawi, funds were sent to assist 28 families who lost their homes, possession and crops. This project was brought to life because the families with young unschooled children in this part of Namwera, Malawi, needed a school which is within walking distance. This is a very poor under-served area of Malawi. There are no schools nearby for young children and where there is a primary school, many little girls are not encouraged or given the opportunity to attend. As a result of the Malawi Project in Namwera the children will have an opportunity to learn to read, write, and compute in Chichewa and English - the two official languages of Malawi.

OUR MISSION

  • > To encourage and support learning in young children.
  • > To ensure that the connection between nutrition and learning is understood.
  • > To provide as many opportunities as possible to improve the quality of life for all residents.
  • > To offer support for skills development which have an entreprenurial component.
