The Future of Learning in Namwera

October 28, 2017, by Kai Gammage

One of the greatest signs of success that the Namwera Project can point to is just how much the school has grown since its inception. It's been made clear that children want to learn, the determination in each of their hearts is apparent. Cre- ating a beacon of education that children want to flock towards is ultimately the goal, to promote a sustained desire to learn. With more children from the region signing up year after year, with more families, young men and women, all veering towards this pathway to education, the need for additional amenities has increased. Happy's desire to be a teacher, and to pay that work forward have been a huge addition to the cause. His determination and progress in teachers college are a sign of great things to come. But as class sizes continue to grow, the need for additional help in the classroom remains.

We at the Namwera Project have found a solution, a new innovation in classroom technology: the OneTab. The OneTab is a learning tablet designed to give its users a focused and interactive learning experience tailored to their level of progress and abilities. It's a pre-programmed device that will be able to teach kids in their native Chichewa as well as in English.
It teaches children numeracy, literacy, and even dabbles in expanding their knowledge of the world at large. Another incredible reason why we're looking towards the OneTab as the next step in promoting education in Malosa, is its abili- ty to teach kids about digital literacy.
In an increasingly digitized world, knowing how to navigate new technologies, and having a basic understanding of how something like a touchscreen works, are incredibly valuable. Solving problems on a piece of hardware resembling something like an iPad goes a long way in making sure children are able to use that technology in the future. And more than that, it's even solar powered, with a push towards promoting sustainability at this local level.
They've been used in Malawi before, helping other schools get off the ground, and help the teachers achieve their ed- ucational goals. It has allowed children to work themselves up to a point where the majority of the class is achieving at the same level.
The OneTab is our hopeful next step in our work here in Namwera. We see these tablets as an incredible opportunity to not only help out our students, but help out our teachers too. Granting children and educators the access to new educational technology goes a long way towards securing the future for members of this community.
